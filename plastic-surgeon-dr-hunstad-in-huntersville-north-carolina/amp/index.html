How many hair follicles do we loose during a Facelift done after extreme weight loss?
Can be anesthesia or stress related
Jul 22, 2014
Having hair loss after any
surgery can be anesthesia or stress related. There is always some hair removed
when the excess scalp either above the ear or behind the ear is removed, but if
it is a density situation, this is usually secondary to anesthesia or stress
around the time of surgery and almost always resolves with time. The timeframe
can be anywhere between 3 and 12 months.
I had a facelift two and a half years ago and still feel uncomfortable tightness in neck.
Unusal, but not rare
Jul 22, 2014
Feeling tightness after a face lift even 2-1/2 years ago is
unusual but not rare. Physical therapy is an excellent modality that can help
relieve this. Other non-western modalities such as acupuncture may indeed be
helpful. I don’t believe there is any contraindication at all undergoing it and
if it is helpful, by all means continue.
Why would doctor do a short scar facelift with incision in front of tragus as opposed to behind it?
Incision location is a personal choice
Jul 22, 2014
The decision to make the incision either in front or behind
the tragus, the tab in the center of the ear, is one of personal choice. Either
are considered very reasonable. More and more surgeons have adopted the
retro-tragal approach, because it does tend to at times be less visible, but
some surgeons believe that placing the incision in front of the tragus,
particularly in men, is preferable.
How long does the numbness after a facelift stays?
Numbness after a facelift is variable
Jul 22, 2014
Numbness after a facelift is actually quite variable.
Certain areas such as around the mouth usually lose no sensation, whereas the
area immediately in front of the ear tends to take the longest time for normal
feeling to occur. In the majority of cases we see, that feeling for the most
part has usually returned within 6 weeks or so. Some areas certainly can take
longer, but 6 weeks seems to be a reasonable time period.

Jul 22, 2014
At this point the tissue that was sewn back on your nose
does look compromised, but it still has some coloration in the center. It is
often amazing how much of this replanted skin can survive. Most physicians are
very conservative in their care with this by just doing dressing changes,

it. You will be amazed how much actually does survive, and in the long term how
good it can look. Initially they do look terrible and they often look somewhat

Removal of Metal Plates from Face?
Serious facial injury
Jul 22, 2014
You have certainly had a very serious facial injury
requiring multiple plates and fixation. As healing goes on however, many of
these plates can be removed safely after there is evidence that the bone itself
has healed. In your community, a number of doctors can address many complex
facial fractures with plates, including plastic surgeons. Sometimes oral and
maxillofacial surgeons and ear, nose and throat surgeons also can treat
patients with facial fractures and similar deformities.
Paranasal Implants to Help with Bone Loss?
Para nasal implants will not help any bone loss, but can camouflage bone loss
Jul 22, 2014
Para nasal implants will not
help any bone loss, but can camouflage bone loss and are quite valuable in that
area. These implants are somewhat unusual, not nearly as common as chin
implants or cheek implants, but can be beneficial in this situation. After the
swelling has resolved following surgery, additional fillers or fat grafting may
be necessary to fully blend and achieve the desired result you are looking for.

I have asymmetry after face/neck lift. What are the my chances of it resolving itself?
Certain irregularities are common
Jul 22, 2014
Certain irregularities are common in the early
weeks after face lifting. Many times we advocate massage, ultrasound and other
therapies to help improve this during the healing phase. Full healing takes a
good 3 months and at that point things usually are fairly fixed. The
irregularities of the neck may improve with conservative means. Occasionally
those do persist and may require some additional lifting.

How do plastic surgeons feel about the transaxillary incision?
Transaxillary incision
Aug 18, 2020
In our office we do not offer this type of incision. This incision often widens over time and doesn't heal the best. This is then seen in bathing suits, tank tops, and dresses. In our office we use a IMF incision which is located in the crease of the breast. The only way this incision is seen is if you put your arms up over your head.

How many cm can one deviate from its BWD while choosing implants?
BWD deviation
Aug 7, 2020
In our office we tell patients to stay within 1.5cm of their base width. You don't want an implant that is to small and doesn't fill out the width of your chest but you also don't want an implant that is to wide and will make you look top heavy. If your base width is 12cm I would lean more toward the 375cc implant with a base width of 11.75cm. I would discuss this further with your board certified plastic surgeon.
What size would take me to a D cup from 36 B?
From B to D cup size
Aug 7, 2020
We often tell our patients that a cup size is approximately 200cc. So in theory you need around a 400cc implant to go from a B to a D. I think that if you were thinking of a 385cc implant moving up to a 405cc or 420cc implant is very reasonable. I would reach out to your board certified plastic surgeon and discuss your options.
Do breast augmentations hurt more when you don't have breast fat or tissue?
Breast augmentation pain
Aug 7, 2020
Breast augmentation doesn't hurt more if you have a small amount of breast tissue. However, if there is a small amount of breast tissue present we will suggest putting the implant behind the muscle to help the implant look more natural. This can cause more discomfort in the first few days of your recovery. You should contact a board certified plastic surgeon in your area for a full in person consultation.

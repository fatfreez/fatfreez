Equipment for Coolsculpting treatment

The equipment for cryogenic liposuction was developed by Zeltiq and at present has no analogues. Other devices for cryolipolysis should not be used.

The full name of the apparatus for cryolipolysis is Zeltiq Coolsculping. The company produces six types of applicators for processing different parts of the body:

  • CoolCurve+;
  • CoolMax;
  • CoolCore;
  • CoolFit;
  • CoolSmooth.

Vacuum applicators CoolCurve and CoolCurve + are used to handle small folds located in hard-to-reach places and on bends of the body.

The CoolCore and CoolFit applicators are designed to affect the fat folds in the back, abdomen, the sides and the inside of the thighs. Large folds are processed using the largest CoolMax nozzle. The most progressive applicator is CoolSmooth. This modification of the applicator does not create a vacuum, its plates firmly adhere to the skin, which virtually eliminates the occurrence of side effects.

Indications

Coolsculpting is recommended for patients who have fat deposits on the inner surface of the arms and legs, at the waist, in the abdomen, on the back. The main indication for the procedure is obesity of the alimentary-constitutional type, associated with low motor activity and eating disorders.

Coolsculpting is also indicated in the following cases:

  • endocrine obesity associated with violations of hormonal regulation in the body;
  • psychological obesity in people who are prone to “jamming” of stress;
  • obesity, associated with violations of the functions of the digestive system, the cause of which are disruptions in the work of the hypothalamic-pituitary system.
  • Corrections through cold liposculpturing are subject to the deposition of fat in places that are inaccessible to other devices used in the practice of aesthetic medicine, a zone where carrying out other cosmetic procedures is difficult due to the peculiarities of the structure and not amenable to correction by physical exercises.

Contraindications

Contraindications for carrying out coolsculpting are:

  1. Raynaud’s syndrome;
  2. increased sensitivity to cold;
  3. pregnancy and lactation;
  4. presence of an electrocardiostimulator;
  5. recently transferred operations (until complete healing of scars);
  6. presence of open wounds and scars of traumatic origin in the zone subject to correction;
  7. mental illness.

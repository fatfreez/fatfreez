Cryo Before And After

Physicians have developed a new technique for painless and harmless weight reduction is cryolipolysis. It refers to the most effective and safe procedures for correcting the body shape.

Cryo treatment is an innovative method of hardware cooling with the help of a special device, as a result of which the amount of fat deposits decreases.

The principle of cryolipolysis

The basis is the sensitivity of fat cells to low temperatures: the cold destroys them, the liquid enters the space between the skin cells, is naturally removed from the body.

The process starts when adipose tissue cooling to -5 degrees and lasts for two months. During this time there is a gradual decrease in the treated fat layer by 20-40%.

The procedure starts with applying a cooling gel to the problem area, the fat fold is drawn in and cooled by a vacuum nozzle without pain and discomfort.

CRYO-FREEZE Is The Alternative To Liposuction

The session lasts about an hour, several problem areas are processed. Next, you need a break about a month.

The result will remain for life, subject to proper nutrition, healthy lifestyle.

There are 2 types nozzels:

  • With a vacuum nozzle, after which hematomas and bruises are possible.
  • With the metal plates, do not lead to the appearance of bruises.

This method perfectly copes with excess fat deposits in the most problematic parts of the body: buttocks, abdomen, back, waist, knees and face.

However, after correction of the body shape according to this technique, side effects are possible:

  • Burning, itching on the skin.
  • Loss of skin sensitivity for several weeks.
  • Convulsions.
  • Hematomas.

Contraindications to cryo treatment

Before Coolsculpting treatment, you need to undergo a medical examination. There are a number of contraindications:

Cryo-freeze Is A Non-invasive Procedure

  • hypersensitivity to cold;
  • Raynaud’s disease;
  • skin diseases;
  • neuropathic disorders;
  • pregnancy and lactation;
  • presence of an electrocardiostimulator;
  • kidney failure;
  • diabetes;
  • hormonotherapy;
  • disorders of the endocrine system;
  • diseases of the gallbladder and liver;
  • Obesity II and III degree;
  • disorders of the circulatory system.

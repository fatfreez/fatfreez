To date, cryolipolysis is the most effective and popular procedure for painless removal of fat and cellulite.

Cryolipolysis has a lot of positive properties and is absolutely safe.

It is worth noting that fat cells are destroyed not at the time of direct exposure, but for several months after Coolsculpting.

As the number of fat cells decreases, their thickness is also reduced by 20-25%. As you know, adipose tissue is especially sensitive to cold.

It begins to break down when the temperature drops to minus five degrees. Lipolysis is achieved by crystallization of fat cells.

Technology of the procedure

Before the procedure for cryolipolysis, specialists assess the size of the fat layer. Depending on the severity of the problem and the most problematic zone, one of the four applicators is selected.

After that, the applicator and the gel pad are applied to the problem area. Under the influence of vacuum, the applicator draws a fold of fat and gradually cools the subcutaneous fat. The duration of one Coolsculpting session is one hour. All that the patient experiences is a little pressure and tingling.

Please note: there are two types of apparatus for carrying out cryolipolysis: with a vacuum nozzle and with a flat metal surface. The choice of this or that option depends on the severity of the problem and individual indications.

After the applicator is removed, the skin in the “capture” area turns red, it may even turn blue a little, like after using “medical jars”. In the initial state, the skin returns after 20-30 minutes.

How much does cryolipolysis cost?

The cost of the procedure depends on many factors. This, first of all, the degree of severity of the problem and the area of carrying out cryolipolysis. On average, the price of Coolsculpting is 500 to 4000$.

Is there any harm from the Coolsculpting procedure?

The procedure of cryolipolysis can be accompanied by certain side effects and complications. In the first minutes after coolsculpting, there may be a slight discomfort.

Also among the most common complications are:

  • Edema.
  • Hematomas.
  • Bruises.
  • Tingling.
  • Burning sensation.
  • Convulsions.
  • Temporary loss of skin sensitivity.

Body Contouring Without Surgery

Also note: cryolipolysis, like all other procedures, has contraindications:

  • Injuries of the skin.
  • Presence of hernias or abundant scars in the treatment zone.
  • Exacerbation of skin diseases.
  • Individual intolerance to cold.
  • Diabetes.
  • Pregnancy and lactation.
  • Mental disorders.
  • Hepatic and renal insufficiency.
  • The presence of a pacemaker.
  • Obesity of II and III degree.
  • Thrombosis.
  • Diseases of the circulatory system.
  • Predisposition to bleeding.

The advantages of Coolsculpting

In just one session of cryolipolysis, you can get rid of ¼ of fat. The course of coolsculpting consists of several procedures. In a few weeks you will notice the first results.

Cool Sculpting Works Essentially By Freezing Targeted Areas Of Fat

Cryolipolysis is a highly effective procedure that involves programmed cell death. This is a natural and constant process. The device only accelerates this process, but not so drastically. At the same time, the integrity of the skin is not disturbed. Also it should be noted that coolsculpting practically does not load the liver and kidneys.

The volume of fat deposits is reduced gradually and evenly. At the same time, the skin does not hang at all and does not stretch. If necessary, coolsculpting can be combined with other procedures.

For cryolipolysis, virtually all areas of the body are available. The only thing – the skin in the place of impact should be captured in the crease.

Thus, it is drawn into the cooling applicator. The procedure is not accompanied by damage to the cell membranes. Also there is no risk of side effects (hematomas, pain, swelling).

So, cryolipolysis is a qualitatively new method of affecting fat cells. After the course of treatment you simply do not recognize your body.

CoolMini: CoolSculpting for Double Chin

At our practice, we use the coolsculpting mini applicator on the double chin area to eliminate small, stubborn pockets of unwanted fat. Sometimes, however, patients who come in to see us for CoolSculpting are actually concerned about skin laxity or looseness.

Having an in-person consultation with a board certified plastic surgeon in your area will help you determine the type of treatment option that would work best for you. CoolSculpting Under The Chin

If your main issue is fat accumulation under your chin then the new CoolSculpting CoolMini applicator can be used to eliminate the unwanted fat. If there is loose skin then other treatments may be better.

Often it is a combination of fat and loose skin and therefore combination treatments may be needed. I would recommend finding a physician who has multiple different options for treating this area. After a consultation and examination they will be able to determine which treatments are best for you. (Michael A. Zadeh, MD, FACS, Sherman Oaks General Surgeon)

Cooslculpting Mini Applicator For Double Chin

coolsculpting selectively freezes fat cells to induce programmed cell death. A new applicator has been designed to treat under the chin. However, treatment does not address skin and muscle laxity. For this reason, you may benefit from an in person consultation with a board certified facial plastic surgeon to determine which procedure is appropriate for you. (Suzanne Kim Doud Galli, MD, PhD, FACS, Washington Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Can CoolSculpting be used on the neck and under chin?

CoolSculpting under the chin

There is an applicator specially made to treat the chin area. If you are assessed and have a pocket of fat in the area you may be a candidate. It will eliminate the fat in the area.

However, if you have concerns with skin laxity in the neck CoolSculpting is not the treatment to correct those needs. (Robert G. Aycock, MD, FACS, Bay Area Plastic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting and the neck

The Cool mini is the new piece which can be used under the neck to freeze the fat. Some patients prefer Kybella for this area as well. Either method is very effective. (Michele S. Green, MD, New York Dermatologist)

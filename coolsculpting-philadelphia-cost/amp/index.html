Cryolipolysis is a non-surgical procedure, during which fat cells are destroyed after purposeful cooling.

The mechanism of action of Coolsculpting treatment

This technique is based on a different degree of cell resistance to low temperatures. It is due to the properties of the cells that make up the tissues, the different rates of blood supply, metabolism and oxygen consumption by cells.

For example, skin cells tolerate cooling well and recover quickly, while subcutaneous fat cells do not have protective mechanisms against cold.

A Safe And Effective Way To Reduce Fat In Common Trouble Areas

With significant cooling, the cells are irreversibly damaged, gradually killed and transported to the lymphatic system and processed during metabolism.

This feature of cells underlies cryolipolysis.

During the cryolipolysis session, a helium gasket and an applicator are applied to the problem area. Fat fold on the problem area is captured by a vacuum applicator inside the nozzle and is subjected to intensive cooling between two cooling plates at a temperature of -5 Â° C.

How long does the cryolipolysis session last?

The session lasts 60 minutes and it is absolutely comfortable. During the procedure, the patient can freely listen to music, read, work on a laptop. After the end of the procedure, you can immediately return to the rhythm of your daily life.

When will the results of the procedure be visible?

This process, as a rule, takes from 6 to 8 weeks. The amount of adipose tissue in the problem areas decreases from 2-4 cm.

Can the result be improved by repeating the procedures?

One procedure is sufficient to achieve an expressed result. However, in the problem areas it is recommended to repeat the procedure in 30-45 days. The most stable areas are the fat deposits on the back. Here, it is possible to repeat the cryolipolysis up to three times. Belly and leg sites are considered the most favorable for this therapy.

Indications for coolsculpting treatment:

Cryolipolysis is recommended for excess fat deposits on the abdomen, back, waist, hips, legs and hands, as well as cellulite and flabbiness of the skin. The procedure is carried out on all parts of the body, except the face, neck and decollete zone. The main condition is the presence of a fat layer at least 1-2 cm.

What are the side effects and risks of coolsculpting treatment?

The cryolipolysis technique is absolutely safe. Cryolipolysis affects only fat cells. Cold damage to the skin, blood vessels, nerves, muscles or internal organs is excluded.

How long does the recovery last?

Restrictions after the procedure are minimal. Rehabilitation is not required, you can go to work or go driving. Also there are no restrictions on playing sports. However, visiting the sauna for one week is undesirable.

Is cryolipolysis equally effective for all patients?

Based on our experience, 5-7% of patients are resistant to this procedure. This is due to many reasons, such as ethnic, sexual and hormonal. Can cryolipolysis replace surgical liposuction?

With cryolipolysis, fat cells are not completely destroyed, and only a part of the cells are stimulated to self-destruct â apoptosis. The thickness of the fat layer gradually decreases. This method is very good for small body fat.

During coolsculpting, fat cells are eliminated mechanically. Therefore, cryolipolysis is not a substitute for liposuction. In addition, the effect of skin tightening, which is present after liposuction, does not accompany cryolipolysis.

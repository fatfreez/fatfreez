Cryogenic Lipolysis Results

Cryogenic lipolysis is a unique method of eliminating excess fat deposits by acting at a low temperature, as a result of which the amount of adipose tissue slowly but surely decreases, and the destruction of fat cells lasts about 2-4 months after the procedure.

During all this time, the volume of fat decreases and the result obtained in the problem areas will be noticeable, and yet it will look natural, giving a smooth silhouette to the body as a whole. To achieve greater results, the cryogenic lipolysis procedure can be repeated after 1-1.5 months.

During the procedure, up to 25% of fat deposits in the waist, back, and thighs are destroyed. This is a very effective procedure with a lasting result.

A Newly Popular Fat Reduction Technique

Are certain areas/body compositions more responsive to Cryogenic lipolysis than others?

The cryogenic lipolysis easily copes with fatty deposits, which can not be eliminated even with the help of intensive physical training and strict diets. It is worth noting that the result can be saved for a long time with regular physical activity and proper nutrition. The most effective cryogenic lipolysis will be for the following sites of fat accumulation:

  1. stomach
  2. waist
  3. knees
  4. thighs
  5. back.

Cryogenic lipolysis has a number of positive properties, including safety, non-invasiveness, painlessness (the procedure is carried out without the slightest injury to the skin), no toxic effect, and high accuracy of cold on fat deposits.

After cryogenic lipolysis

Sometimes there is a slight itching or numbness due to cold exposure, and slight redness after the procedure. All these manifestations are short-lived and will not affect the usual way of life, to which you can return immediately.

Contraindications to the procedure cryogenic lipolysis

Contraindications to the appointment of cryolipolysis are the following factors:

  • circulatory disorders in the area of exposure to cold (blood clotting pathology, thrombosis, etc.), the use of anticoagulants; violations of skin sensitivity;
  • recent trauma in the treatment, including with damage to the blood vessels (bleeding, hemorrhage);
  • open and / or infected wounds, inflammatory processes, recent surgical manipulations in the area of cold treatment;
  • severe skin lesions (extensive scar) or subcutaneous tissue (hernia) in the affected area;
  • pathological sensitivity to cold;
  • pregnancy and lactation;
  • presence of a pacemaker.

This method uses advanced cold exposure technology to selectively target fat deposits and destroy fat cells by a gradual process that, without harming surrounding tissues, removes unwanted fat. When the fat cells are subjected to precise cold exposure, they begin the process of natural removal of fat, which gradually reduces the thickness of the fat layer.
